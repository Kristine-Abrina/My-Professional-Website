Interactive Cat Map of Manila Planned Project

Kristine H. Abrina

Status: In Planning Phase Type: Community Web Application

Project Overview

Inspired by the "I Know Where Your Cat Lives" Website


The Interactive Cat Map of Manila is a planned community-driven platform designed to connect cat lovers, volunteers, and caregivers across Metro Manila. This web application aims to create a comprehensive visual database of community cats throughout the city, enabling residents to document, track, and care for stray and community cats in their neighborhoods. The map will serve as a collaborative space where users can share photos, update feeding schedules, post observations, and coordinate care efforts for specific cats or cat colonies. By visualizing cat populations across Manila, the platform seeks to raise awareness about urban wildlife, promote responsible community cat care, and facilitate connections between volunteers and animal welfare advocates. The project envisions a gamified approach with a leaderboard system that recognizes active contributors and caregivers, encouraging sustained community engagement. Additionally, the platform will allow the promotion of donation links to support feeding programs and veterinary care for the documented cats, creating a sustainable ecosystem for community-based animal welfare initiatives in Manila.

Planned Features

  • Interactive Manila Map: Dynamic map interface highlighting locations of documented community cats across different areas of Metro Manila
  • Photo Pin System: Visual markers on the map displaying cat photos as pins, making it easy to browse and locate specific cats in different neighborhoods
  • Cat Profile Pages: Clicking on map pins opens detailed profiles containing photo galleries, visitor comments, health updates, and location-specific information for each cat or cat colony
  • Community Comments & Updates: Interactive comment sections where volunteers and residents can share observations, health concerns, behavioral notes, and other relevant updates about specific cats
  • Volunteer Feeding Log: Collaborative feeding schedule tracker where community members can log when and what they fed specific cats, helping coordinate care and prevent overfeeding or gaps in care
  • Donation Integration: Direct links to fundraising pages or donation platforms dedicated to supporting individual cats or cat colonies with veterinary care, food, and shelter needs
  • Caretaker Leaderboard: Gamified recognition system ranking the most active volunteers and caregivers based on feeding logs, photo contributions, and community engagement metrics
  • Search and Filter Functionality: Tools to search for cats by location, color, size, or other characteristics, and filter map views by specific areas or cat colonies
  • User Authentication: Secure login system for contributors to maintain feeding logs, post updates, and build community reputation through verified contributions
  • Mobile-Responsive Design: Optimized interface for smartphones to enable on-the-go documentation and updates from volunteers in the field

Planned Technologies
